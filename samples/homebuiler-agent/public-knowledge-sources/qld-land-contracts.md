## Purpose

Provide a practical, non‑legal explanation of Queensland land contracts, focusing on elements that affect building feasibility, design, and site costs.

---

## What a Land Contract Covers

A Queensland land contract outlines the terms under which a buyer purchases a residential lot.  
It typically includes:

- Contract of sale  
- Disclosure plan  
- Easements  
- Covenants  
- Building envelopes  
- Settlement conditions  
- Sunset dates  
- Developer obligations  

This information helps builders prepare accurate quotes and identify constraints early.

---

## Key Components of a QLD Land Contract

### Disclosure Statement

Required under the **Land Sales Act 1984 (QLD)**.  
Includes:

- Lot dimensions  
- Easements  
- Services  
- Slope/contours  
- Retaining walls  
- Access points  

Public source: <https://www.qld.gov.au/law/housing-and-neighbours/building-and-renovating>

---

### Easements

Easements may restrict:

- Building location  
- Driveway placement  
- Fencing  
- Landscaping  

Common easements include:

- Sewer  
- Stormwater  
- Water  
- Access  
- Electricity  

Public source: <https://www.resources.qld.gov.au/land/property/land-titles/easements>

---

### Covenants

Covenants are developer‑imposed rules that may specify:

- Façade requirements  
- Roof pitch  
- Materials  
- Fencing style  
- Landscaping  
- Driveway materials  

These override council rules and affect design choices.

Public source: <https://www.stockland.com.au/residential/qld>

---

### Building Envelopes

Some estates define:

- Maximum building area  
- Setbacks  
- Height limits  
- Private open space  
- Garage placement  

These constraints directly affect home design.

---

### Services & Infrastructure

Contracts often show:

- Sewer connection point  
- Water meter location  
- Stormwater outlet  
- NBN pit location  

This influences:

- House orientation  
- Plumbing layout  
- Driveway design  

---

### Retaining Walls

Contracts may specify:

- Developer‑built retaining  
- Height  
- Location  
- Responsibility for maintenance  

Retaining walls can significantly affect site costs.

---

### Settlement Conditions

Common conditions include:

- Registration of the plan of subdivision  
- Developer works completion  
- Sunset dates  
- Finance clauses (if applicable)  

These affect build timelines.

---

## Why Land Contracts Matter for Building

Land contracts reveal constraints that impact:

- Build feasibility  
- Engineering requirements  
- Site costs  
- Design limitations  
- Compliance with covenants  
- Driveway and service placement  

Builders rely on accurate contract information to avoid variations later.

## Purpose

Provide a clear, practical explanation of Queensland’s Home Warranty Insurance (HWI) scheme so users understand what is covered, what is not, and how claims work.

---

## What Home Warranty Insurance Is

Home Warranty Insurance (HWI) is a mandatory insurance scheme administered by the QBCC.  
It protects homeowners if:

- The builder dies  
- The builder disappears  
- The builder becomes insolvent  
- The builder fails to rectify defective work  
- The builder fails to complete the project  

Public source: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/home-building-owners/home-warranty-insurance>

---

## When HWI Applies

HWI applies to:

- New homes  
- Renovations over $3,300  
- Townhouses  
- Duplexes  
- Major structural work  
- Swimming pools (structural components)  

The builder must pay the premium **before starting work**.

---

## What HWI Covers

### Non-completion

If the builder cannot complete the home due to insolvency, death, or disappearance, HWI may cover:

- Completion costs  
- Additional costs to engage a new builder  
- Loss of deposit (up to limits)  

---

### Defective Work

Covers defects such as:

- Structural defects  
- Waterproofing failures  
- Major cracking  
- Roof leaks  
- Faulty installation of key components  

Coverage periods:

- **6 years 6 months** for structural defects  
- **12 months** for non-structural defects  

---

### Subsidence or Movement

If caused by defective work, HWI may cover:

- Slab movement  
- Footing failure  
- Structural cracking  

---

### Damage Caused by the Builder

Includes:

- Damage to the property  
- Damage to neighbouring properties  
- Damage to services  

---

## What HWI Does NOT Cover

HWI does not cover:

- Wear and tear  
- Maintenance issues  
- Damage caused by the owner  
- Design issues (unless builder-provided)  
- Variations not documented in the contract  
- Non-compliant work done by unlicensed contractors hired by the owner  

---

## Claim Process

1. Lodge a claim with the QBCC  
2. QBCC assesses eligibility  
3. QBCC may inspect the site  
4. Builder may be issued a Direction to Rectify  
5. If unresolved, QBCC may approve a payout or appoint a completion contractor  

Public guidance:  
<https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/home-building-owners/home-warranty-insurance/claiming-insurance>

---

## Premiums

Premiums are:

- Paid by the builder  
- Based on contract value  
- Mandatory before work starts  

The homeowner can request proof of payment.

---

## Red Flags for Consumers

- Builder refuses to provide proof of HWI  
- Builder asks the owner to pay the premium directly  
- Builder starts work before insurance is issued  
- Contract value is artificially lowered to reduce premium  

These are strong indicators of risk.

## Summary

Home Warranty Insurance is one of the strongest consumer protections in Queensland.  
It provides coverage for non-completion, defective work, and structural issues, helping homeowners manage risk when building or renovating.

## Planning Hierarchy in Queensland

Queensland uses a layered planning system:

1. **State Planning Instruments**  
   - State Planning Policy (SPP)  
   - Regional Plans (e.g., SEQRP)  
   - Temporary Local Planning Instruments (TLPI)

2. **Local Government Planning Schemes**  
   - Zoning  
   - Overlays  
   - Neighbourhood plans  
   - Development codes

3. **Site-Specific Instruments**  
   - Covenants  
   - Easements  
   - Design guidelines (common in new estates)

This hierarchy determines what can be built on a site, how it must be designed, and what constraints apply.

## Key Land Concepts

### Zoning

Defines the primary land use (e.g., Low Density Residential, Medium Density Residential, Rural, Emerging Community).

### Overlays

Identify constraints or special considerations such as:

- Flood hazard  
- Bushfire hazard  
- Slope/landslide  
- Biodiversity  
- Character/heritage  
- Transport noise corridors  

### Lot & Plan Numbers

Every property in Queensland is identified by a **Lot** and **Plan** number (e.g., Lot 12 on RP123456).  
These are used for title searches, easements, and development assessments.

### Disclosure Plans (New Estates)

For new land releases, developers must provide:

- Lot dimensions  
- Easements  
- Services  
- Slope  
- Retaining walls  
- Covenants  
- Building envelopes

## Purpose

Explain what a disclosure plan is, why it matters in Queensland land purchases, and how to interpret the key elements that affect building feasibility and cost.

---

## What a Disclosure Plan Is

A disclosure plan is a mandatory document provided by developers for new land subdivisions in Queensland.  
It outlines essential information about the lot so buyers understand constraints before signing a contract.

Disclosure plans are required under the **Land Sales Act 1984 (QLD)**.

---

## What Disclosure Plans Typically Include

### Lot Dimensions

Shows:

- Frontage  
- Depth  
- Total area  
- Irregular boundaries  

Important for:

- Minimum frontage rules  
- Driveway placement  
- House design fit  

---

### Contours & Slope

Indicates:

- Natural ground levels  
- Fall direction  
- Height differences  

Slope affects:

- Retaining walls  
- Slab type  
- Site costs  
- Drainage design  

Public source: <https://qldglobe.information.qld.gov.au/>

---

### Easements

Common easements include:

- Stormwater  
- Sewer  
- Water  
- Access  
- Electricity  

Easements may restrict:

- Building location  
- Driveway placement  
- Fencing  
- Landscaping  

Public source: <https://www.resources.qld.gov.au/land/property/land-titles/easements>

---

### Services

Shows where utilities enter the lot:

- Sewer connection point  
- Water meter location  
- Stormwater outlet  
- NBN pit  

This affects:

- House orientation  
- Plumbing runs  
- Driveway design  

---

### Retaining Walls

Indicates:

- Developer-built retaining  
- Height and location  
- Whether walls are engineered  

Retaining walls can:

- Limit building envelopes  
- Affect fencing  
- Increase site costs if additional walls are needed  

---

### Building Envelopes

Some estates include:

- Setback requirements  
- Maximum building area  
- Height limits  
- Private open space requirements  

These override standard council rules.

---

### Covenants & Design Guidelines

Disclosure plans often reference estate-specific rules such as:

- Façade requirements  
- Roof pitch  
- Materials  
- Fencing style  
- Landscaping  

Public source: <https://www.stockland.com.au/residential/qld>

---

## Why Disclosure Plans Matter

They help identify:

- Build feasibility  
- Potential site costs  
- Design limitations  
- Engineering requirements  
- Hidden constraints  

Builders rely on disclosure plans to prepare accurate quotes.

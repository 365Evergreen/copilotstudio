## Purpose

Provide a practical, non‑copyrighted summary of soil classifications used in Queensland residential construction, based on publicly available guidance from QBCC and QLD Government sources.

---

## Why Soil Classification Matters

Soil type determines:

- Slab design  
- Footing depth  
- Engineering requirements  
- Site costs  
- Risk of movement or cracking  

Queensland has a high proportion of reactive clay soils, especially in SEQ.

---

## Soil Classes (Public Summaries)

### Class A — Acceptable

- Little to no ground movement  
- Sand and rock sites  
- Least expensive slab requirements  

Common in: coastal sandy areas.

---

### Class S — Slightly Reactive

- Small ground movement  
- Minor seasonal moisture changes  

Common in: some SEQ coastal suburbs.

---

### Class M — Moderately Reactive

- Moderate ground movement  
- Requires stiffer slabs  

Common in: Brisbane, Logan, Ipswich, Moreton Bay.

---

### Class H1 — Highly Reactive (Level 1)

- High ground movement  
- Requires reinforced slabs  
- More expensive engineering  

Very common across SEQ.

---

### Class H2 — Highly Reactive (Level 2)

- Very high ground movement  
- Deep footings or edge beams required  
- Increased slab costs  

Common in: Ipswich, Springfield, Ripley, parts of Logan.

---

### Class E — Extremely Reactive

- Extreme ground movement  
- Significant engineering required  
- Highest slab cost before Class P  

Found in: pockets of black soil regions.

---

### Class P — Problem Site

Reasons include:

- Fill  
- Soft soils  
- Uncontrolled fill  
- Slope  
- Landslip risk  
- Trees close to the building envelope  

Requires a geotechnical engineer and custom slab design.

---

## Factors That Influence Soil Class in Queensland

- Depth of fill  
- Moisture variation  
- Tree roots  
- Clay content  
- Slope  
- Drainage patterns  

Reactive clay soils expand when wet and shrink when dry, causing movement.

---

## Engineering Implications

Higher-reactivity soils may require:

- Deeper footings  
- Wider edge beams  
- Additional steel reinforcement  
- Screw piers or bored piers  
- Moisture control systems  

Builders typically include allowances based on assumed soil class until a soil test confirms the actual classification.

## Summary

Soil classification is one of the biggest drivers of slab design and site costs in Queensland.  
Understanding soil classes helps users anticipate engineering requirements and avoid unexpected variations.

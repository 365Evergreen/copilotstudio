## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of the site costs typically encountered when building a home in Queensland, why they vary between builders, and how users can interpret allowances and estimates.

---

## What Site Costs Are

Site costs are expenses required to prepare the land for construction.  
They are separate from the base house price and vary significantly depending on:

- Soil type  
- Slope  
- Fill depth  
- Drainage  
- Retaining  
- Service locations  
- Engineering requirements  

Queensland’s varied terrain means site costs can differ widely even within the same estate.

---

## Major Components of Site Costs

### Earthworks

Includes:

- Cut and fill  
- Levelling the building platform  
- Removal of excess soil  
- Importing fill if required  

Costs increase with:

- Steeper slopes  
- Deep fill  
- Poor access  

---

### Retaining Walls

Retaining may be required when:

- The lot has significant fall  
- Neighbouring lots are at different levels  
- The building platform needs stabilisation  

Retaining walls can be:

- Timber  
- Concrete sleeper  
- Blockwork  
- Engineered systems  

Retaining is one of the largest variable costs in Queensland.

---

### Slab Upgrades

Slab design depends on soil classification.  
Higher-reactivity soils (H1, H2, E, P) require:

- Deeper footings  
- Wider edge beams  
- Additional reinforcement  
- Screw piers or bored piers  

Public summary: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/home-building-owners/building-or-renovating/soil-classification>

---

### Piering

Piering is required when:

- Fill depth exceeds engineering limits  
- Soil is unstable  
- The site has reactive clay  
- The slab needs additional support  

Common in new estates with deep fill.

---

### Drainage

Drainage costs include:

- Stormwater pipes  
- Connection to the legal point of discharge (LPOD)  
- Surface drains  
- Subsoil drains  
- Overland flow management  

Lots affected by overland flow or poor fall require more extensive drainage.

Public source: <https://www.qld.gov.au/environment/water/stormwater>

---

### Service Connections

Includes:

- Sewer connection  
- Water connection  
- Stormwater outlet  
- NBN conduit  
- Power pillar connection  

Costs increase when:

- Services are far from the building envelope  
- Sewer depth is shallow or deep  
- Driveway crosses service lines  

---

### Driveway & Crossover

Driveway costs vary based on:

- Slope  
- Length  
- Material  
- Council crossover requirements  

Steep driveways may require engineering.

---

### Site Access

Difficult access increases costs for:

- Machinery  
- Material delivery  
- Crane use  
- Temporary works  

Examples:

- Narrow lots  
- Corner lots  
- Shared driveways  

---

## Why Site Costs Vary Between Builders

Builders may use different assumptions for:

- Soil class  
- Fill depth  
- Retaining requirements  
- Drainage design  
- Engineering allowances  
- Standard inclusions  

Some builders include minimal allowances to appear cheaper upfront.

---

## Red Flags for Consumers

- Very low site cost allowances  
- Missing allowances for known risks (slope, fill, drainage)  
- Builder refusing to provide engineering assumptions  
- Large number of Provisional Sums  
- No soil test before contract signing  

These can indicate a high likelihood of variations later.

---

## How to Reduce Site Cost Risk

- Obtain a soil test early  
- Request engineering plans before contract signing  
- Ask for realistic allowances  
- Confirm retaining responsibilities  
- Review the disclosure plan and contour map  
- Ask for unit rates (e.g., $/m³ for excavation)

## Summary

Site costs are one of the most variable and misunderstood components of building a home in Queensland.  
Understanding the drivers behind earthworks, retaining, drainage, and slab design helps users anticipate realistic costs and avoid unexpected variations.

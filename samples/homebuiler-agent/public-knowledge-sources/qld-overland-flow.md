## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of overland flow in Queensland, how it is assessed, and how it impacts home design, drainage, engineering, and build cost.

---

## What Overland Flow Is

Overland flow is surface water that flows across land during intense rainfall events.  
It is **not** the same as river or creek flooding.

Overland flow typically occurs when:

- Rainfall exceeds the capacity of stormwater systems  
- Water flows downhill across multiple properties  
- Local drainage is insufficient  
- Impervious surfaces increase runoff  

Public source: <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building/planning-guidelines-and-tools/flood-awareness-map>

---

## How Overland Flow Differs from Flooding

### Overland Flow

- Caused by heavy rain  
- Short duration  
- Fast-moving, shallow water  
- Localised to specific streets or lots  

### Flooding (River/Creek)

- Caused by waterway overflow  
- Longer duration  
- Deeper water  
- Affects larger catchments  

Overland flow can occur even when a property is not in a flood zone.

---

## Why Overland Flow Matters for Building

Overland flow can affect:

- House placement  
- Slab height  
- Drainage design  
- Retaining walls  
- Driveway levels  
- Landscaping  
- Fencing design  

Builders must ensure the home does not obstruct natural flow paths.

---

## How Overland Flow Is Assessed

Assessment typically includes:

- Contour analysis  
- Local council mapping  
- Stormwater network capacity  
- Flow path modelling  
- Neighbouring lot levels  
- Driveway and crossover gradients  

Local councils often provide overland flow mapping.

Examples:

- Brisbane: <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building/planning-guidelines-and-tools/flood-awareness-map>  
- Gold Coast: <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building>

## Common Overland Flow Solutions

### Raising the House

Increasing slab height to avoid water ingress.

### Overland Flow Paths

Designated channels or swales that allow water to pass through the lot.

### Surface Drains

Includes:

- Spoon drains  
- Channel drains  
- Yard drains  

### Subsoil Drains

Used to manage groundwater and reduce saturation.

### Retaining Wall Drainage

Retaining walls must include:

- Weep holes  
- Agg pipes  
- Gravel backfill  

### Driveway Design

Driveways may require:

- Crossfall  
- Spoon drains  
- Grated drains  

---

## Engineering Requirements

Engineers may require:

- Hydraulic assessments  
- Minimum floor levels  
- Flow path preservation  
- Non-obstructive fencing  
- Retaining wall drainage design  

These requirements vary by council.

---

## How Overland Flow Affects Build Cost

Costs may increase due to:

- Additional drainage  
- Higher slab levels  
- Retaining wall drainage  
- Regrading of the site  
- Specialist engineering  
- Driveway drainage solutions  

Overland flow is one of the most common causes of unexpected site cost variations.

---

## Red Flags for Consumers

- Builder not checking overland flow mapping  
- No drainage plan provided  
- House placed directly in a flow path  
- Retaining walls without drainage  
- Driveway sloping toward the house  
- Underpriced allowances for drainage  

These can lead to water ingress and costly variations.

## Summary

Overland flow is a critical site consideration in Queensland.  
It affects drainage, slab height, retaining walls, and overall build cost.  
Understanding flow paths early helps avoid variations and ensures compliant, resilient home design.

## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of how the QBCC handles complaints, disputes, and disciplinary actions so users can understand builder risk indicators and how to interpret public records.

---

## What the QBCC Oversees

The QBCC manages:

- Building disputes  
- Defective work investigations  
- Unlicensed work complaints  
- Contract issues  
- Home warranty insurance claims  
- Disciplinary actions against builders  

Public source: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/>

---

## Types of Complaints the QBCC Handles

### Defective or Incomplete Work

Covers issues such as:

- Structural defects  
- Waterproofing failures  
- Poor workmanship  
- Incomplete work  

---

### Contractual Disputes

Includes:

- Variations  
- Delays  
- Payment disputes  
- Contract interpretation issues  

---

### Unlicensed Work

QBCC investigates:

- Builders operating without a licence  
- Contractors performing work outside their licence class  

---

### Financial Misconduct

Includes:

- Insolvency  
- Exceeding turnover limits  
- Failing to meet MFR requirements  

---

## QBCC Disciplinary Actions

The QBCC may take disciplinary action when a builder:

- Performs defective work  
- Breaches contract obligations  
- Fails to meet financial requirements  
- Engages in unlicensed work  
- Fails to comply with directions to rectify  

Actions may include:

- Fines  
- Licence conditions  
- Licence suspension  
- Licence cancellation  

Public register: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/home-building-owners/complaints-and-disputes/disciplinary-actions>

---

## Public Registers and What They Show

### Licence Search Register

Shows:

- Licence status  
- Conditions  
- Suspensions  
- Expiry dates  
- Trading names  

<https://my.qbcc.qld.gov.au/s/contractor-lookup>

---

### Disciplinary Register

Shows:

- Formal disciplinary decisions  
- Reasons for action  
- Penalties applied  
- Dates of decisions  

This is one of the strongest trust signals available.

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Multiple disciplinary actions  
- Recent licence suspensions  
- Conditions relating to financial capacity  
- Repeated complaints for similar issues  
- Sudden changes in trading names  
- Builder refusing to provide licence details  

These indicators suggest elevated risk.

---

## How the Complaint Process Works

1. **Lodge a complaint** with the QBCC  
2. QBCC assesses eligibility  
3. QBCC may inspect the site  
4. Builder may be issued a Direction to Rectify  
5. If unresolved, the matter may escalate to:  
   - Home warranty insurance  
   - QCAT (Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal)  

Public guidance: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/home-building-owners/complaints-and-disputes>

---

## Summary

QBCC complaints and disciplinary records provide essential insight into a builder’s reliability and compliance history.  
Understanding how to interpret these public records helps users identify trustworthy builders and avoid unnecessary risk.

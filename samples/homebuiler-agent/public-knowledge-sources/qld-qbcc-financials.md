## Purpose

Provide a clear, practical explanation of the QBCC Minimum Financial Requirements (MFR) framework so users can understand how builder financial reporting works and why it matters for risk assessment.

---

## What the MFR Is

The **Minimum Financial Requirements (MFR)** are rules that ensure Queensland builders have:

- Sufficient working capital  
- Sustainable debt levels  
- Adequate net tangible assets  
- Capacity to complete contracted work  

The MFR helps protect consumers from builder insolvency.

Public source: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/licensing/minimum-financial-requirements>

---

## Why the MFR Matters

Builders must meet financial thresholds to:

- Obtain a QBCC licence  
- Renew their licence  
- Increase their allowable turnover  
- Avoid licence suspension  

If a builder fails to meet MFR obligations, the QBCC may:

- Impose conditions  
- Reduce allowable turnover  
- Suspend the licence  
- Cancel the licence  

These are strong trust signals for consumers.

---

## Key Components of the MFR

### Net Tangible Assets (NTA)

Builders must maintain a minimum level of NTA based on their turnover category.  
NTA excludes:

- Intangible assets  
- Goodwill  
- Related-party loans (unless secured)  

---

### Maximum Revenue (MR)

Each builder is assigned a maximum annual turnover they are allowed to perform.  
If they exceed it, they must lodge updated financials.

---

### Working Capital

Builders must demonstrate sufficient liquidity to meet short-term obligations.

---

### Debt Levels

Excessive debt or poor liquidity can trigger:

- Licence conditions  
- Increased reporting  
- Suspension  

---

## Financial Categories

The QBCC groups builders into categories based on annual turnover.

### Category 1

- Up to $800,000 turnover  
- Common for small builders and trades  

### Category 2

- $800,001 to $3 million  
- Many boutique builders fall here  

### Category 3–7

- $3 million to $240 million+  
- Larger project builders and commercial operators  

Public source: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/licensing/minimum-financial-requirements>

---

## Reporting Obligations

Builders must lodge:

- Annual financial information (AFI)  
- MFR reports when turnover increases  
- Updated financials when conditions change  

Failure to lodge can result in licence suspension.

---

## Red Flags in MFR Compliance

- Licence conditions relating to financial capacity  
- Sudden changes in turnover category  
- Suspended or cancelled licence  
- Frequent trading name changes  
- Disciplinary actions linked to financial issues  

These are strong indicators of potential risk.

---

## How Consumers Can Use MFR Information

The public QBCC register shows:

- Licence conditions  
- Suspensions  
- Restrictions on turnover  
- Financial monitoring flags  

Public register: <https://my.qbcc.qld.gov.au/s/contractor-lookup>

This helps users assess builder stability before signing a contract.

---

## Summary

The QBCC MFR framework is one of the most important consumer protection mechanisms in Queensland.  
Understanding financial categories, turnover limits, and licence conditions helps users identify stable, well‑managed builders.

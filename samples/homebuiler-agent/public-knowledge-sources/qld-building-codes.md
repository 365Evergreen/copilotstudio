## Purpose

Provide a practical overview of the building codes and standards that apply to residential construction in Queensland, using only publicly available summaries and government guidance.

---

## How Building Codes Work in Queensland

Queensland construction is governed by a combination of:

- National Construction Code (NCC)  
- Queensland Development Code (QDC)  
- Local planning schemes  
- State planning instruments  
- Bushfire and flood requirements  
- Engineering standards (summaries only)  

Builders must comply with all applicable codes when designing and constructing new homes.

---

## National Construction Code (NCC)

The NCC sets minimum requirements for:

- Structural integrity  
- Fire safety  
- Health and amenity  
- Energy efficiency  
- Plumbing and drainage  

Public overview: <https://abcb.gov.au/ncc-overview>

The full NCC is copyrighted, but the ABCB provides free public summaries.

---

## Queensland Development Code (QDC)

The QDC contains Queensland-specific variations to the NCC.  
Key parts relevant to residential building include:

- MP 1.1 — Design and siting for single detached housing  
- MP 1.2 — Design and siting on small lots  
- MP 1.3 — Design and siting for duplexes  
- MP 2.1 — Fire safety  
- MP 4.1 — Sustainable buildings  

Public source: <https://www.hpw.qld.gov.au/services/building/planning/queensland-development-code>

---

## Bushfire Construction Requirements (AS 3959 Summary)

Queensland uses the Bushfire Attack Level (BAL) system.  
BAL levels influence:

- External materials  
- Glazing  
- Vents and openings  
- Decking materials  
- Gutter guards  

Public summary: <https://www.qfes.qld.gov.au/prepare/bushfire/building-in-bushfire-prone-areas>

---

## Slab and Footing Requirements (AS 2870 Summary)

Slab design depends on soil classification.  
Higher-reactivity soils require:

- Deeper footings  
- Wider edge beams  
- Additional reinforcement  

Public summary: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/home-building-owners/building-or-renovating/soil-classification>

---

## Flood-Resilient Construction

Flood overlays may require:

- Minimum habitable floor levels  
- Flood-resilient materials  
- Structural tie-downs  
- Restrictions on ground-floor habitable rooms  

Public guidance: <https://www.qra.qld.gov.au/>

---

## Energy Efficiency Requirements

Queensland homes must meet minimum energy performance standards, including:

- Insulation  
- Glazing  
- Shading  
- Ventilation  
- Roof colour and reflectivity  

Public overview: <https://www.qld.gov.au/housing/building-home/energy-efficiency>

---

## Local Planning Scheme Codes

Local councils add additional requirements such as:

- Setbacks  
- Site coverage  
- Building height  
- Character overlays  
- Small-lot housing rules  

Examples:

- Brisbane City Plan: <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building>  
- Gold Coast City Plan: <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building>  

---

## Why Codes Matter for Builders and Homeowners

Building codes influence:

- Design feasibility  
- Material selection  
- Engineering requirements  
- Build cost  
- Compliance and certification  
- Insurance eligibility  

Understanding the basics helps users interpret builder quotes and avoid unexpected variations.

## Purpose

Explain how overlays work in Queensland planning schemes and how they affect residential building, site costs, and design constraints.

---

## What Overlays Do

Overlays identify **constraints**, **risks**, or **special planning considerations** that apply to a site.  
They often override zoning and can trigger additional assessments or building requirements.

Overlays commonly influence:

- Engineering requirements  
- Bushfire or flood construction standards  
- Site layout and building envelopes  
- Retaining walls and earthworks  
- Stormwater management  
- Vegetation protection  
- Character/heritage controls  

---

## Common Overlays in Queensland

### Flood Hazard Overlay

Indicates flood-prone land and may require:

- Minimum floor levels  
- Flood-resilient materials  
- Engineering reports  
- Limitations on ground-floor habitable rooms  

Public source: <https://qldglobe.information.qld.gov.au/>

---

### Bushfire Hazard Overlay

Applies to bushfire-prone areas and may require:

- BAL (Bushfire Attack Level) assessment  
- Fire-resistant materials  
- Asset protection zones  
- Access and water supply requirements  

Public source: <https://www.qfes.qld.gov.au/prepare/bushfire/building-in-bushfire-prone-areas>

---

### Slope / Landslide Overlay

Identifies steep or unstable land.  
May require:

- Geotechnical reports  
- Engineered retaining walls  
- Specific slab types (e.g., H or P class)  
- Limitations on cut/fill  

Public source: <https://qldglobe.information.qld.gov.au/>

---

### Biodiversity / Environmental Overlay

Protects vegetation, wildlife corridors, and ecological areas.  
May restrict:

- Clearing  
- Earthworks  
- Building envelopes  
- Driveway locations  

Public source: <https://environment.des.qld.gov.au/>

---

### Transport Noise Corridor Overlay

Applies near major roads, rail, or airports.  
May require:

- Acoustic insulation  
- Glazing upgrades  
- Mechanical ventilation  

Public source: <https://www.qld.gov.au/housing/buying-owning-home/noise-corridors>

---

### Character / Heritage Overlays

Common in Brisbane and older suburbs.  
May restrict:

- Demolition  
- Extensions  
- Façade changes  
- Rooflines and materials  

Public source: <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building/heritage-and-character>

---

## How Overlays Affect Building Costs

Overlays can increase costs due to:

- Additional engineering  
- Higher-spec materials  
- Specialist reports  
- Bushfire or flood compliance  
- Retaining and earthworks  
- Acoustic treatments  

Builders often adjust designs or allowances based on overlay requirements.

Understanding them helps users anticipate engineering requirements, risks, and potential cost impacts.

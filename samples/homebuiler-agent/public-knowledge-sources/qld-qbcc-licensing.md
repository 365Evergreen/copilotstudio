## Purpose

Provide a clear, practical explanation of QBCC licensing so users can understand how to verify a builder, what licence classes mean, and what trust signals to look for.

---

## What the QBCC Is

The **Queensland Building and Construction Commission (QBCC)** is the state regulator for:

- Builder licensing  
- Home warranty insurance  
- Dispute resolution  
- Building standards compliance  
- Financial reporting for builders  

Public source: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/>

---

## Why Licensing Matters

A builder must hold the correct QBCC licence to:

- Enter into a building contract  
- Perform residential construction work  
- Supervise construction  
- Claim insurance  
- Provide warranties  

Unlicensed work is illegal in Queensland.

---

## Types of QBCC Licences

### Builder – Low Rise

Allows construction of:

- Houses  
- Townhouses  
- Duplexes  
- Small residential buildings up to 2 storeys  

This is the most common licence for project home builders.

---

### Builder – Medium Rise

Allows construction of:

- Buildings up to 3 storeys  
- Larger townhouse complexes  
- Some commercial buildings  

---

### Builder – Open

Allows construction of:

- Any building of any height  
- High-rise apartments  
- Commercial towers  

---

### Trade Contractor Licences

Examples:

- Carpentry  
- Bricklaying  
- Concreting  
- Waterproofing  
- Plumbing  
- Electrical  

These do **not** allow a contractor to build a house.

---

## How to Verify a Builder

Users can check a builder’s licence using the public QBCC register:

<https://my.qbcc.qld.gov.au/s/contractor-lookup>

The register shows:

- Licence class  
- Licence status (active/suspended)  
- Expiry date  
- Disciplinary history  
- Licence conditions  
- Trading names  

This is one of the strongest trust signals available.

---

## Licence Status Types

### Active

Builder is licensed and compliant.

### Suspended

Builder cannot legally perform or contract building work.

### Cancelled

Licence has been revoked or not renewed.

### Conditions Applied

Builder may have restrictions such as:

- Maximum contract value  
- Supervision limits  
- Financial monitoring  

---

## Red Flags in Licensing

- Suspended or cancelled licence  
- Frequent changes in trading names  
- Conditions relating to financial capacity  
- History of disciplinary action  
- Licence class not matching the work being offered

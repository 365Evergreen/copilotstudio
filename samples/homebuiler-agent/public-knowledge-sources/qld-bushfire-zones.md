## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of bushfire hazard zones and Bushfire Attack Level (BAL) ratings in Queensland, including how they affect home design, materials, and build cost.

---

## What Bushfire Zones Are

Bushfire zones identify areas at risk of bushfire.  
In Queensland, these are mapped through:

- State bushfire hazard mapping  
- Local council overlays  
- QFES bushfire prone area maps  

Public source: <https://www.qfes.qld.gov.au/prepare/bushfire/building-in-bushfire-prone-areas>

---

## How Bushfire Zones Affect Building

If a property is in a bushfire-prone area, a **BAL assessment** is required.  
The BAL rating determines:

- Construction materials  
- Glazing requirements  
- Ember protection  
- Vent and opening treatments  
- Decking materials  
- Gutter guard requirements  

Higher BAL ratings increase build cost.

---

## Bushfire Attack Level (BAL) Ratings

BAL ratings are defined in AS 3959 (public summaries only).  
They measure the expected level of bushfire exposure.

### BAL–LOW

- Minimal risk  
- No special construction requirements  

---

### BAL–12.5

- Ember attack risk  
- Basic ember protection required  
- Some upgraded materials  

---

### BAL–19

- Increased ember attack  
- Higher risk of radiant heat  
- Upgraded glazing and materials  

---

### BAL–29

- High radiant heat  
- Toughened glass required  
- Non-combustible materials in key areas  

---

### BAL–40

- Very high radiant heat  
- Increased risk of flame contact  
- Significant construction upgrades  

---

### BAL–FZ (Flame Zone)

- Direct flame contact possible  
- Highest construction requirements  
- Specialist design and materials  
- Significant cost impact  

---

## Factors That Influence BAL Rating

- Distance to vegetation  
- Vegetation type  
- Slope of the land  
- Fire danger index  
- Surrounding topography  

These factors are assessed by a qualified bushfire consultant.

---

## Common Construction Requirements (Public Summaries)

Depending on BAL rating, requirements may include:

- Toughened or double glazing  
- Metal mesh screens  
- Non-combustible cladding  
- Sealed vents  
- Bushfire-rated doors  
- Gutter guards  
- Enclosed subfloors  
- Non-combustible decking  

Public summary: <https://www.qfes.qld.gov.au/prepare/bushfire/building-in-bushfire-prone-areas>

---

## How Bushfire Zones Affect Build Cost

Higher BAL ratings increase cost due to:

- Upgraded windows and doors  
- Non-combustible materials  
- Additional sealing and screening  
- Specialist engineering  
- Bushfire-rated roofing components  

BAL–29 and above can add significant cost.

## How to Check Bushfire Risk

### Queensland Government

- QFES bushfire mapping: <https://www.qfes.qld.gov.au/prepare/bushfire/building-in-bushfire-prone-areas>

### Local Councils

Most councils include bushfire overlays in their planning schemes.

Examples:

- Brisbane City Plan: <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building>  
- Gold Coast City Plan: <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building>  

---

## Red Flags for Consumers

- Builder not checking BAL rating  
- No bushfire assessment on a bushfire-prone site  
- Quote missing BAL-related upgrades  
- BAL–FZ sites without specialist design  
- Underpriced allowances for glazing  

These can lead to major variations later.

---

## Summary

Bushfire zones and BAL ratings are critical factors in Queensland home design.  
They influence materials, engineering, and build cost, and must be assessed early to avoid unexpected variations.

## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of the planning and building approval process in Queensland, including how overlays, assessments, and certifications affect home design and build timelines.

---

## Overview of the Approval Process

Building a home in Queensland typically requires:

1. **Developer approval** (if in an estate)  
2. **Planning approval** (if required by council)  
3. **Building approval** (mandatory)  
4. **Engineering certification**  
5. **Inspections during construction**  
6. **Final certification (Form 21)**  

Not all projects require planning approval, but all require building approval.

---

## Planning Approval (Council)

Planning approval is required when a proposal does not meet:

- Zoning requirements  
- Overlays  
- Setbacks  
- Site coverage limits  
- Height limits  
- Character or heritage controls  

Public source: <https://planning.statedevelopment.qld.gov.au/>

---

## Common Overlays in Queensland

Overlays may trigger additional requirements for:

- Flooding  
- Overland flow  
- Bushfire  
- Landslide  
- Biodiversity  
- Coastal protection  
- Character housing  
- Small-lot housing  

Examples:

- Brisbane City Plan: <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building>  
- Gold Coast City Plan: <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building>  

---

## Building Approval (Private Certifier)

Most building approvals in Queensland are issued by **private certifiers**, not councils.

Certifiers assess:

- NCC compliance  
- QDC compliance  
- Structural engineering  
- Energy efficiency  
- Fire safety  
- Plumbing and drainage plans  

Public overview: <https://www.qld.gov.au/housing/building-home/building-approvals>

---

## Documentation Required for Building Approval

Typical documents include:

- Site plan  
- Floor plans  
- Elevations  
- Engineering drawings  
- Soil test  
- Energy report  
- Drainage plan  
- Covenant approval (if applicable)  
- BAL assessment (if applicable)  
- Flood level assessment (if applicable)  

---

## Engineering Certification

Engineers provide certification for:

- Slab and footing design  
- Retaining walls  
- Structural framing  
- Stormwater design  
- Driveway gradients (if required)  

Engineering must comply with:

- NCC  
- QDC  
- AS 2870 (slabs and footings)  
- AS 3600 (concrete structures)  
- AS 4100 (steel structures)  

Public summaries only.

---

## Inspections During Construction

Certifiers conduct mandatory inspections at key stages:

- Footings  
- Slab  
- Frame  
- Final inspection  

Additional inspections may be required for:

- Retaining walls  
- Drainage  
- Waterproofing  

---

## Final Certification

Once construction is complete, the certifier issues:

- **Form 21 — Final Inspection Certificate**  

This confirms the home complies with all applicable codes and approvals.

---

## How Approvals Affect Build Cost and Design

Approvals may require:

- Higher slab levels (flood/overland flow)  
- Bushfire-rated materials  
- Additional engineering  
- Setback adjustments  
- Height or site coverage changes  
- Retaining wall redesign  

These can significantly impact cost and feasibility.

---

## Red Flags for Consumers

- Builder not checking overlays  
- No planning assessment provided  
- Missing engineering documentation  
- Certifier not engaged early  
- No flood or BAL assessment on affected lots  
- Design non-compliant with setbacks or envelopes  

These can cause delays, redesigns, or approval refusals.

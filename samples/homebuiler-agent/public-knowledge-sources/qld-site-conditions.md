## Purpose

Provide a practical overview of the site conditions that most affect residential building in Queensland, including engineering requirements, site costs, and design constraints.

---

## Why Site Conditions Matter

Site conditions influence:

- Slab design  
- Retaining walls  
- Drainage  
- Earthworks  
- Build feasibility  
- Construction cost  
- Engineering reports required  

Queensland’s varied geography means site conditions can differ significantly even within the same suburb.

---

## Key Site Condition Factors

### Slope

Slope affects:

- Cut and fill requirements  
- Retaining walls  
- Drainage  
- Driveway gradients  
- Slab type (e.g., H, P class)  

Lots with more than 1–1.5m fall often require engineered retaining.

Public source: <https://qldglobe.information.qld.gov.au/>

---

### Soil Type

Soil classification follows AS 2870 (public summaries only).  
Common Queensland soil classes:

- **S** — Slightly reactive  
- **M** — Moderately reactive  
- **H1/H2** — Highly reactive  
- **E** — Extremely reactive  
- **P** — Problem site (fill, soft soils, slope, etc.)

Reactive soils increase slab costs and may require deeper footings.

Public source: <https://www.qbcc.qld.gov.au/home-building-owners/building-or-renovating/soil-classification>

---

### Fill & Imported Material

Many new estates use engineered fill.  
Key considerations:

- Depth of fill  
- Compaction certificates  
- Whether the builder requires additional piering  

Fill over 400–600mm often triggers extra engineering.

---

### Drainage & Stormwater

Important factors:

- Legal point of discharge (LPOD)  
- Overland flow paths  
- Easements  
- Stormwater pits  
- Natural fall of the land  

Poor drainage can increase site costs significantly.

Public source: <https://www.qld.gov.au/environment/water/stormwater>

---

### Retaining Walls

Retaining may be:

- Developer-built  
- Partially built  
- Required by the builder  
- Required by council  

Retaining walls affect:

- Fencing  
- Neighbour obligations  
- Site access  
- Engineering costs  

---

### Access & Site Constraints

Builders assess:

- Truck access  
- Crane access  
- Material delivery points  
- Narrow frontages  
- Corner lots  
- Shared driveways  

Difficult access can increase build costs.

---

### Services

Key service considerations:

- Sewer connection depth and location  
- Water meter placement  
- Stormwater outlet  
- NBN pit location  
- Power pillar location  

These influence house orientation and plumbing layout.

## Summary

Site conditions are one of the biggest drivers of engineering requirements and site costs in Queensland.  
Understanding slope, soil type, drainage, retaining, and services helps users anticipate build complexity and avoid unexpected variations.

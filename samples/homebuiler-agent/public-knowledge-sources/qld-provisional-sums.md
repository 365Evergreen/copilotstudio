## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of Provisional Sums (PS) and Prime Cost (PC) items in Queensland building contracts, including how they work, why they exist, and how they impact final build cost.

---

## What Provisional Sums Are

A **Provisional Sum (PS)** is an allowance for work where:

- The scope is not fully known  
- The builder cannot accurately price the work yet  
- Site conditions may vary  
- Engineering requirements are unknown  

Examples:

- Rock excavation  
- Retaining walls  
- Drainage upgrades  
- Piering  
- Site cuts and fills  

PS items are **estimates only**, not fixed prices.

---

## What Prime Cost Items Are

A **Prime Cost (PC)** item is an allowance for materials where the exact product is not selected yet.

Examples:

- Tiles  
- Tapware  
- Appliances  
- Vanities  
- Door hardware  

PC items cover **supply only**, not installation.

---

## Why PS and PC Items Exist

They are used when:

- The builder cannot know the exact cost upfront  
- The owner has not selected final materials  
- Engineering reports are pending  
- Site conditions are unknown  
- Council requirements may change  

They allow the contract to proceed without delaying the build.

---

## How PS and PC Items Affect Cost

### If the actual cost is higher

The owner pays the difference.

### If the actual cost is lower

The owner receives a credit.

### If the builder underestimates

This can lead to:

- Variations  
- Budget blowouts  
- Disputes  

---

## Common PS Items in Queensland

- Earthworks  
- Retaining walls  
- Drainage  
- Piering  
- Rock excavation  
- Driveways  
- Stormwater upgrades  
- Service connections  

These are common because Queensland sites often have:

- Reactive soils  
- Slope  
- Fill  
- Overland flow paths  

---

## Common PC Items in Queensland

- Tiles  
- Carpet  
- Tapware  
- Appliances  
- Lighting  
- Bathroom accessories  
- Kitchen fixtures  

These vary widely in price depending on owner selections.

---

## Red Flags for Consumers

- Very low PS allowances  
- Missing PS items for known risks (e.g., slope, fill)  
- PC allowances below realistic retail prices  
- Builder refusing to disclose unit rates  
- PS items used for standard inclusions  

These can indicate a quote designed to appear cheaper than it is.

---

## How to Reduce PS and PC Risk

- Request realistic allowances  
- Ask for unit rates (e.g., $/m³ for excavation)  
- Provide early selections for PC items  
- Obtain soil tests and engineering early  
- Ask the builder to firm up PS items where possible

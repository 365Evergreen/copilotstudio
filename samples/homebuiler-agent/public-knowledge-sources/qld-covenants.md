## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of estate covenants and design guidelines in Queensland, how they work, and how they influence home design, materials, approvals, and build cost.

---

## What Covenants Are

Covenants are private rules imposed by developers to control the appearance and quality of homes in new estates.  
They are **contractual obligations**, not council laws.

Covenants may apply to:

- Façade design  
- Materials  
- Colours  
- Fencing  
- Landscaping  
- Driveways  
- Roof pitch  
- Building envelopes  

Public examples: <https://www.stockland.com.au/residential/qld>

---

## Why Developers Use Covenants

Covenants aim to:

- Maintain estate aesthetics  
- Protect property values  
- Ensure consistent design quality  
- Create a cohesive streetscape  

They often exceed council requirements.

---

## Common Covenant Requirements in Queensland

### Façade Requirements

May include:

- Minimum number of façade elements  
- Rendered or masonry features  
- Timber or feature cladding  
- Window proportions  
- Entry portico requirements  

---

### Roof Requirements

Common rules:

- Minimum roof pitch (e.g., 22.5°)  
- Approved roof materials (e.g., Colorbond)  
- Restrictions on dark roof colours  
- Eaves requirements  

---

### Materials and Colours

Covenants may restrict:

- Bright colours  
- Reflective materials  
- Certain cladding types  
- Excessive use of one material  

---

### Fencing Requirements

Often specify:

- Maximum height  
- Approved materials  
- Front fencing restrictions  
- Side and rear fencing style  
- Setbacks from the front boundary  

---

### Landscaping Requirements

May include:

- Minimum number of trees  
- Turf requirements  
- Garden bed placement  
- Driveway landscaping  
- Completion deadlines  

---

### Driveways

Common rules:

- Approved materials (e.g., exposed aggregate)  
- Colour restrictions  
- Maximum width  
- No plain concrete in some estates  

---

### Building Envelopes

Covenants may define:

- Setbacks  
- Maximum building area  
- Garage placement  
- Private open space  

These override standard council rules.

---

## How Covenants Affect Home Design

Covenants can influence:

- Façade selection  
- Roof design  
- Window placement  
- Material choices  
- Landscaping plans  
- Driveway design  

Builders must ensure the design complies before submitting for developer approval.

---

## Developer Design Approval Process

Most estates require:

1. Preliminary design submission  
2. Developer review  
3. Feedback or required changes  
4. Final approval  
5. Council building approval  

Developer approval must occur **before** building approval.

---

## How Covenants Affect Build Cost

Costs may increase due to:

- Upgraded façades  
- Higher roof pitches  
- Non-standard materials  
- Landscaping requirements  
- Driveway material restrictions  
- Additional design work  

Some covenants require premium materials that exceed standard builder inclusions.

---

## Red Flags for Consumers

- Builder not reviewing covenants before quoting  
- Quote missing covenant-related upgrades  
- Façade non-compliant with estate rules  
- Roof pitch below covenant minimum  
- Driveway material not allowed  
- Landscaping allowances too low  

These can lead to costly variations or design rejections.

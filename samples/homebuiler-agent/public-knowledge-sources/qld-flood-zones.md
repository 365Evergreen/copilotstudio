## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of flood zones and flood planning requirements in Queensland, including how they influence home design, engineering, and build cost.

---

## What Flood Zones Are

Flood zones identify land that may be affected by:

- River flooding  
- Creek flooding  
- Overland flow  
- Storm tide inundation  
- Localised flooding  

Flood information is mapped by:

- Local councils  
- Queensland Reconstruction Authority (QRA)  
- State Government datasets  

Public source: <https://www.qra.qld.gov.au/>

---

## Types of Flooding in Queensland

### Riverine Flooding

Occurs when rivers overflow due to heavy rainfall.  
Common in:

- Brisbane River  
- Logan River  
- Bremer River  
- Mary River  

---

### Creek Flooding

Occurs in smaller catchments and can rise quickly.  
Often affects suburban areas.

---

### Overland Flow

Surface water flowing across land during intense rainfall.  
Common in:

- Brisbane  
- Gold Coast  
- Sunshine Coast  

Public source: <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building/planning-guidelines-and-tools/flood-awareness-map>

---

### Storm Tide / Coastal Inundation

Caused by:

- Cyclones  
- Storm surge  
- High tides  

Affects coastal and low‑lying areas.

---

## Flood Overlays in Planning Schemes

Local councils include flood overlays that may:

- Restrict building locations  
- Require minimum floor levels  
- Limit ground-floor habitable rooms  
- Require flood-resilient materials  
- Trigger additional engineering  

Examples:

- Brisbane City Plan Flood Overlay: <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building>  
- Gold Coast Flood Overlay: <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building>  

---

## Flood Planning Levels (FPL)

The **Flood Planning Level (FPL)** is the minimum height at which habitable floors must be built.

FPL is usually based on:

- 1% AEP flood level (1-in-100-year event)  
- Plus a freeboard allowance (typically 300–500mm)  

Builders must design homes so that:

- Habitable floors sit above the FPL  
- Non-habitable areas may be allowed below FPL  
- Materials below FPL are flood-resilient  

---

## How Flood Zones Affect Home Design

Flood overlays may require:

- Raising the house  
- Split-level designs  
- Flood vents  
- Flood-resilient materials  
- Enclosed or open undercroft areas  
- Restrictions on ground-floor bedrooms  

These requirements can significantly affect cost and design options.

---

## Engineering Requirements

Depending on flood risk, engineering may include:

- Structural tie-downs  
- Elevated floor systems  
- Water-resistant materials  
- Ventilation for undercroft areas  
- Flood flow path management  

Engineers must certify compliance with local flood codes.

---

## How Flood Zones Affect Build Cost

Costs may increase due to:

- Elevated construction  
- Additional engineering  
- Flood-resilient materials  
- Restricted slab types  
- Stormwater upgrades  
- Site access challenges  

Flood-affected lots often require more complex design solutions.

## Red Flags for Consumers

- Builder not checking flood overlays  
- No FPL assessment provided  
- Quote missing flood-related engineering  
- Ground-floor bedrooms on flood-affected lots  
- Underpriced allowances for raising the home  

These can lead to major variations later.

---

## Summary

Flood zones and FPL requirements are critical considerations in Queensland home design.  
They influence engineering, materials, and build cost, and must be assessed early to avoid unexpected variations.

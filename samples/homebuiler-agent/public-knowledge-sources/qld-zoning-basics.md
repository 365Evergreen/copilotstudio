## Purpose

Provide a clear explanation of zoning in Queensland, how it works, and how it affects residential building outcomes.  
This helps the Homebuilder Copilot guide users through land selection, constraints, and builder requirements.

---

## What Zoning Does

Zoning determines:

- What can be built  
- How dense development can be  
- Building height limits  
- Site coverage  
- Setbacks  
- Whether a development application (DA) is required  

Zoning is defined in each council’s **Planning Scheme**, but the structure is broadly consistent across Queensland.

---

## Common Residential Zones in Queensland

### Low Density Residential (LDR)

- Standard suburban housing  
- Detached dwellings  
- Lower building heights (typically 1–2 storeys)  
- Lower site coverage  

### Medium Density Residential (MDR)

- Townhouses, duplexes, small unit buildings  
- Higher site coverage  
- More flexible setbacks  

### High Density Residential (HDR)

- Apartments  
- Taller buildings  
- Urban centres and transit corridors  

### Emerging Community (EC)

- Future residential development areas  
- Often used in new estates  
- May require additional assessments or structure plans  

### Rural Residential (RR)

- Large lots  
- Acreage homes  
- Limited services and infrastructure  

---

## How Zoning Affects a New Home Build

Zoning influences:

- Whether your home design is compliant  
- Whether a DA is required  
- Maximum building height  
- Minimum lot size  
- Setbacks and site coverage  
- Whether dual occupancy is allowed  
- Whether small-lot housing rules apply  

Builders often adjust designs based on zoning constraints.
